What is a celebration of life?

A celebration of life is a less formal alternative to a traditional funeral ceremony. It focuses on honoring and celebrating the happy memories and positive impact the departed person had during their lifetime. These ceremonies can be held anywhere and at any time that feels right for family and friends.

Unlike traditional funerals, celebrations of life are typically more personalized and less structured. They might include sharing stories, displaying photos, playing favorite music, or participating in activities the person enjoyed.

The goal is to create a meaningful tribute that reflects the unique personality and legacy of your loved one.

When can you hold a celebration of life?

That's up to you.

Families tell us that they love that the timing for a celebration of life is flexible and needn't depend on a loved one's burial or cremation. Though many celebrations take place right before or right after these events, others take place days, weeks or even months afterward. Even families who choose very traditional funeral services may opt to hold more celebratory gatherings afterward.

When deciding when to hold a celebration of life, a family might consider factors like travel schedules, existing commitments, or significant cultural or community events that held meaning for their loved one. Whatever the decision, the date and time of a celebration can be planned to suit your family's needs, and we're available to plan a celebration shortly after a death or for any point in the future.

A celebration of life brings people together to honor your loved one.

With an emphasis on the good times, happy memories and joyous moments, a celebration of life brings friends and family together in an uplifting and supportive setting where conversation, laughter, hugs and tears are welcome.
